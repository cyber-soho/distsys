Comparison: Types of Blockchain

Who participates? Anyone

Who validates? Anyone (Decentralized)

Examples: Bitcoin, Ethereum

Who participates? Invited members only

Who validates? Designated nodes

Examples: Hyperledger Fabric

Who participates? Selected organizations

Who validates? Pre-approved nodes

Examples: R3 Corda, Quorum

Day 3: Cryptocurrencies & Consensus

Explore the difference between money issued by governments and money issued by algorithms.

Fiat vs. Crypto Comparison

Note: "Centralization" and "Anonymity" are qualitative metrics for comparison.

How do we agree? (Consensus)

Concept: Computational Competition.

Miners compete to solve complex math puzzles. The winner adds the block. Extremely secure but energy-intensive. Used by Bitcoin.

Concept: Economic Stake.

Validators lock up coins as collateral. They are chosen randomly to add blocks. Energy efficient. Used by Ethereum (post-merge).

Concept: Democratic Voting.

Token holders vote for delegates who validate transactions on their behalf. Faster, but arguably less decentralized.
